If \(f(x) = \sinh(x)\) what is \(f'(x)\text{?}\)

\begin{align*} \sinh(x) \amp = \dfrac{1}{2}e^x - \dfrac{1}{2}e^{-x} \\ \dfrac{d}{dx} \left( \sinh(x) \right) \amp = \dfrac{d}{dx} \left( \dfrac{1}{2}e^x - \dfrac{1}{2}e^{-x} \right) \\ \amp = \dfrac{1}{2}e^x - \left( \dfrac{1}{2}e^{-x} \cdot -1 \right) \\ \amp = \dfrac{1}{2}e^x + \dfrac{1}{2}e^{-x} \\ \amp = \cosh(x) \end{align*}
